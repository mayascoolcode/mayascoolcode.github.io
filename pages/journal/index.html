Trying to adhere to a schedule is very hard for someone who has a crazy brain like me. I am hot when I want to be cold, and cold when I want to be hot. This is what it is like trying to accomidate myself to adult life. My executive dysfuntion is chronic and hard to get through. I have found, I cannot have sweets and overindulge in fun things, or else I will loose my mind and reward myself for not doing any work. I dont know how some people are able to do it, really. It's truly a mystery to me. I am trying to make my plans less linear, and more accomidating to my silly self. It's a lot of work, but I am determined to keep trying. I am also working on my emotional intelligence. I have a hard time identifying my own feelings sometimes, I recently found a this emotion wheel that helps me figure things out. This is very...idk...autistic. I feel that might be a possibility, but I am unsure. People really throw that word around these days, so I'd like to be sure. If I am, I am high masking for sure, as I am learning how to differentiate between certain parts of myself. I am very unmasked around my partner, which is good. Learning what "happy" feels like, masked and unmasked is very hard. I am used to being over the top and overcompensating for my "lack of emotion" or expressions. I feel like I beat myself up for not being able to sustain that, but I think being open and emotionally honest (with the people who can handle it) will be a good enough start for me.

Hey, welcome to my little blog. Heres an introduction: I'm Amaya S.B. (2004, he/they). I am an artist from California and I enjoy making edgy femme drawings of kemonomimis. I want to share my art online, but the internet stresses me out. So I made my own website, as an archive and time capsule for myself and my works. I first began drawing as a child, a Sonic fanartist. I remember drawing art of Cream and Blaze, I really liked them. Of course as I got older I was introduced to anime and animation memes, and so I started drawing thick furries as a teenager. I started having crazy artblock later on, and would draw a lot of humans. But now, I've found myself at an equilibrium, drawing cute kemonomimi art (more human-ish characters with animal characteristics). I love "girly" things, dark palletes, and textures. As I enter 2026, I want to relax and learn to really enjoy creating again, I've had a very hard time because of pride and perfectionism. I just want to relax now and not take it too serious. I've never seriously shown my art to a audience or anything like that, so I'm gonna take advantage of that fact and allow myself to be silly. A couple more projects I have besides that, is an EP and a cute dress up game. I want to connect to the things I enjoyed doing as a kid, like playing GirlsGoGames and animatics. 2025 was very blah for me. It felt just, like a waste of time, as I once again let my fear get in the way of many things. But there were some good things that have happened for me in the end. I graduated community college and I've found a lovely person despite being hopeless, and having basically zero energy. It was not all bad, at all. I'm excited to see what happens this year.
